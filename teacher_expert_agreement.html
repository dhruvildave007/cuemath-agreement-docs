ANNEXURE B

CODE OF CONDUCT APPLICABLE TO TEACHERS

CUEMATH strongly believes that a Teacher plays a crucial role in nurturing each Student’s potential. Accordingly, this ‘Code of Conduct’ aims to serve as a guiding compass, as Teacher seek to steer an ethical and respectful course while imparting knowledge to Students, and to uphold the honour and dignity of the teaching profession. The ethical foundation for the teaching profession is encapsulated in the values of respect, care, integrity and trust, and the same are reflected throughout the ‘Code of Conduct’.

Each Teacher pledges that, he shall be:

  1. True to the mission of bringing out the best in Students;
  2. Exemplary in the discharge of their duties and responsibilities;
  3. Continue to learn and pass on the love of learning to the Students;
  4. Supportive and co-operative with the Parents, so as to ensure holistic development of the Student.

In light of the above pledge, each Teacher during their engagement with CUEMATH, as a CTE, shall strictly adhere to the below mentioned ‘Code of Conduct’. CUEMATH takes a very serious view of any failure, breach, and/or non-adherence to the ‘Code of Conduct’, and the same shall be dealt with in a manner as detailed below.

  1. 1. CONDUCT TOWARDS THE STUDENTS

    1. Under no circumstances, shall a Teacher be permitted to mentally, emotionally, and/or sexually harass a Student, or cause fear, trauma, anxiety. Further, a Teacher shall not administer any act of corporal punishment, or any act that inflicts physical, mental or emotional pain on the Students, or causes physical, mental or emotional harm to the Students such as pushing, pulling, hitting and/or flogging, as the case may be;
    2. Shall take all reasonable steps in relation to the care of the Students under their supervision, so as to ensure their complete safety and welfare. CTE shall ensure that they do not conduct Teaching Session(s) while under the influence of any alcoholic or narcotic or any other substance which impairs their fitness to conduct the Teaching Session(s), etc.;
    3. Shall not show favoritism towards any particular Student;
    4. Shall not disrespect Students, and/or use foul language with the Students;
    5. Shall treat all the Students equally and with care;
    6. Shall respect the diversity of each Student, including but not limited to the differences arising from gender, family status, religion, age, disability, race, etc.
    7. Shall build a culture of mutual respect during teaching sessions, and shall encourage the Students to respect their Teachers, Parents and one another;
    8. Shall ensure that Students don’t bully or cause mental/ physical injuries to one another, at the teaching sessions or thereafter;
    9. Shall ensure that the Teaching Sessions are conducted, and the Students are taught the Curriculum, in the best possible manner;
    10. Shall ensure that the Curriculum is delivered, in each Teaching Session, in the right manner;
    11. Shall aspire to act as a role model to the Students, and promote holistic development of each Student;
    12. Shall not disrespect the Students who have left his Teaching Session and/or did not enroll with him;
    13. Shall maintain strict confidentiality of all the information pertaining to the Students, Parents and/or other Person connected to CUEMATH and dispense such information only to Persons who are legitimately entitled to the same, as per Applicable Law;
    14. Shall report to CUEMATH, where appropriate, incidents or matters which have, or could potentially have an impact on the Students’ welfare;
    15. Shall communicate effectively with the Students in a manner that is professional, collaborative, supportive and based on mutual trust and respect;
    16. Shall ensure that all communications with the Students is of an appropriate nature as indicated hereinabove, including all communications with the Students through electronic platform or electronic medium including but not restricted to e-mail, messaging, social networking sites;
    17. Shall be open and responsive to constructive feedback regarding their teaching from Enrolled Students and Parents; and
    18. Shall avoid conflict between their professional work and private interests which could reasonably be deemed to impact negatively on Students.
  2. 2. CONDUCT TOWARDS THE PARENTS

    1. Shall conduct Parent Teacher Meetings as per the prescribed format and frequency;
    2. Shall update the progress/ necessary information/ feedback pertaining to the Student(s), for parents to be able to track the progress of their child;
    3. Shall provide objective feedback about their child’s behavior towards their Teacher and classmates;
    4. Shall establish and maintain a culture of mutual trust and respect;
    5. Shall respect the Parent and his child’s individuality, uniqueness and privacy;
    6. Shall update Parents of any change in the CUEMATH’s policy, pricing, Curriculum, etc., if so directed or required by CUEMATH;
    7. Shall encourage Parents to provide any kind of feedback that will help the CUEMATH grow;
    8. Shall encourage Parents to ask questions, share views and participate in development and learning of the Students;
    9. Shall treat all the Parents and children equally and without any prejudice;
    10. Shall be responsive to all reasonable requests in relation to their child’s education;
    11. Shall identify, resolve and immediately inform the CUEMATH about any existing or potential conflict with the Parent;
    12. Shall talk about Parents with respect, every time specially in front of children and other Parents;
    13. Shall not show disrespect to Parents who left CTE or did not enroll with him;
    14. Shall communicate effectively with Parents, in a manner that is professional, collaborative and supportive, and based on trust and respect;
    15. Shall ensure that any communication with Parents is appropriate, including communication through electronic platform or electronic medium including but not restricted to e-mail, messaging, social networking sites; and
    16. Shall be open and responsive to constructive feedback regarding their teaching and, if necessary, seek appropriate advice from the Parents.
  3. 3. CONSEQUENCES OF BREACH/ NON-ADHERENCE TO THE ‘CODE OF CONDUCT’

    1. Upon receipt of a complaint from a Parent, and/or a enrolled Student (hereinafter referred to as ‘Complainant’), against a Teacher, CUEMATH shall initiate an investigation into the same.
    2. A disciplinary authority/ committee shall be constituted by the board of CUEMATH to investigate into the complaint (hereinafter referred to as ‘Disciplinary Committee’). Such Disciplinary Committee shall comprise of 6 (Six) members, (i) Head of Curriculum, (ii) Head of Market Operation, (iii)Head of Columbus Teacher Training, 3 Senior Teachers (to be selected by the Head of Curriculum). Any change in composition of Disciplinary committee would be communicated periodically.
    3. The Disciplinary Committee shall issue a notice to the Teacher, Complainant, and any other Person, who in its opinion should be given an opportunity of being heard in the inquiry, to furnish to the Disciplinary Committee a statement relating to the Complainant. The said statement is to be supported with a list of the documents, if any, and the same is to be forwarded to the Disciplinary Committee, wherever practicable.
    4. The Disciplinary Committee, shall examine all the statements furnished to it under Paragraph 3.3 above.
    5. The Disciplinary Committee shall follow principles of natural justice in all its proceedings and shall as far as possible maintain confidentiality of the identity of the Complainant, the Teacher against whom the complaint has been received, and any witnesses, if any.
    6. Upon completion of the proceeding, the Disciplinary Committee shall pass a reasoned order stating whether the Teacher is guilty or not of the said complaint. A copy of such order shall be made available by the Disciplinary Committee to the Teacher, Complainant, and any other relevant party.
    7. In the event the Teacher is found to be guilty, then the consequences shall be as detailed in this Agreement, including but not limited to written warning, suspension, monetary penalty, immediate termination and/or blacklisting, depending on the nature of the breach/ violation, frequency of the complaints received against the said Teacher, the work record of the Teacher, etc.
    8. The decision of the Disciplinary Committee, in this regard shall be final and binding on all parties to the proceeding.
    9. The Disciplinary Committee shall have the power to regulate its own procedure in respect of any matter for which no provision is made herein above.
